About Me

I am a Ph.D. researcher in Computer Science at the University of North Dakota, specializing in Biomedical Natural Language Processing (BioNLP), Large Language Models (LLMs), and Multimodal Learning. My work focuses on developing state-of-the-art AI solutions for biomedical text and relation mining, with applications in health informatics and bioinformatics.

I design and evaluate BioNLP pipelines using models such as BERT, GPT, Llama, and Gemini, aiming to accelerate discovery in biomedicine through automated literature mining and information extraction. I served as the team lead and first author of a system that was recognized as a runner-up in the BioASQ BioNNE challenge, with our work presented at the CLEF 2024 Conference. I have also presented at the Great Lakes Bioinformatics Conference (GLBIO) and university research symposia.

My first-author publications appear in venues such as Bioinformatics Advances, and I have contributed as a co-author to collaborative studies published in journals like BMC Public Health.

With a strong foundation in machine learning, deep learning, and biomedical AI, I am passionate about bridging computational methods with real-world biomedical challenges to uncover novel insights and advance translational research.
